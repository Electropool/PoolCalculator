 STEP 1 — DOMAIN VERIFICATION IN GOOGLE SEARCH CONSOLE
================================================================================

Google Search Console (GSC) is the most important tool for monitoring and
controlling how Google indexes your site. You MUST set this up before anything
else.

1.1 ACCESS GOOGLE SEARCH CONSOLE
  URL: https://search.google.com/search-console

1.2 ADD YOUR PROPERTY
  Click "Add property" → Choose "Domain" property type (NOT URL prefix).
  
  Enter: poolcalculator.electropool.online
  
  Domain property covers all subdomains and both HTTP/HTTPS. This is the
  most comprehensive option.

1.3 VERIFY DOMAIN OWNERSHIP
  Google will show you a TXT record to add to your DNS.
  
  For Cloudflare (which this site uses):
    a) Open Cloudflare Dashboard → Your domain zone (electropool.online)
    b) Go to DNS → Add Record
    c) Type: TXT
    d) Name: @ (or the subdomain: poolcalculator)
    e) Content: (paste the TXT value Google gives you)
    f) TTL: Auto
    g) Click Save
    h) Return to Google Search Console and click "Verify"
  
  Verification typically takes 5-60 minutes after DNS propagates.

1.4 ADD URL PREFIX PROPERTY TOO (optional but recommended)
  Also add as URL Prefix: https://poolcalculator.electropool.online/
  This gives you more detailed page-level data.
  Verify using the HTML tag method:
    Google gives you: <meta name="google-site-verification" content="XXXX" />
    Add this to public/index.html inside <head>:
      <meta name="google-site-verification" content="PASTE_YOUR_CODE_HERE" />
    Deploy, then click Verify.


================================================================================
 STEP 2 — SUBMIT YOUR SITEMAP
================================================================================

2.1 CONFIRM SITEMAP IS LIVE
  Open in browser: https://poolcalculator.electropool.online/sitemap.xml
  You should see the XML sitemap with all 10 URLs.
  If you see a 404 or blank page, the file was not deployed correctly.
  The file must be at: public/sitemap.xml in the React project root.

2.2 SUBMIT SITEMAP TO GOOGLE SEARCH CONSOLE
  In GSC: Left sidebar → Indexing → Sitemaps
  In the "Add a new sitemap" field, enter:
    sitemap.xml
  (GSC auto-prepends your property URL)
  Click Submit.

2.3 CHECK SITEMAP STATUS
  After submitting, GSC shows:
    Status: "Success" — Google accepted and is processing
    Status: "Couldn't fetch" — Check URL is accessible, not blocked by robots.txt
    Status: "Has errors" — Open the sitemap URL and check for XML syntax errors
  
  Discovered URLs count should show: 10 (all your routes)

2.4 MONITOR SITEMAP COVERAGE
  GSC → Indexing → Pages
  You'll see a breakdown:
    Indexed — pages Google has indexed
    Not indexed — pages Google found but chose not to index
    Crawled, currently not indexed — Google has seen the page but hasn't indexed yet
  
  Target: All 6 calculator pages + homepage indexed = 7 minimum.


================================================================================
 STEP 3 — REQUEST MANUAL INDEXING FOR KEY PAGES
================================================================================

Don't wait for Google to find your pages — request indexing manually for
your most important pages right after submitting the sitemap.

3.1 USE THE URL INSPECTION TOOL
  GSC → Left sidebar → URL Inspection
  Enter each page URL one at a time:

  Priority order:
    1. https://poolcalculator.electropool.online/
    2. https://poolcalculator.electropool.online/resistor-color-code
    3. https://poolcalculator.electropool.online/led-calculator
    4. https://poolcalculator.electropool.online/smd-code
    5. https://poolcalculator.electropool.online/capacitor-code
    6. https://poolcalculator.electropool.online/inductor-code
    7. https://poolcalculator.electropool.online/about
    8. https://poolcalculator.electropool.online/privacy-policy

3.2 FOR EACH URL:
  a) After entering URL, click "Request Indexing"
  b) Google will confirm the request
  c) WAIT: Indexing takes 1-14 days per page (new sites may take longer)
  
  IMPORTANT: You can only request indexing ~10 times per day.
  Space out your requests over 2-3 days.

3.3 WHAT TO LOOK FOR IN URL INSPECTION RESULTS:
  "URL is on Google" — Already indexed, good!
  "URL is not on Google" — Not yet indexed. Click "Request Indexing."
  
  In the "Coverage" section:
    Indexing allowed? → Should say "Indexing allowed"
    User-declared canonical → Should match the URL you entered
    Google-selected canonical → Should match (if different, there's a conflict)
  
  In the "Enhancements" section:
    Structured data — should show your schema types (WebApplication, FAQPage)
    If schema is detected with no errors, this is excellent.


================================================================================
 STEP 4 — VERIFY GOOGLEBOT CAN RENDER YOUR SPA
================================================================================

Because this is a React SPA, you must test that Googlebot can actually read
your content (not just an empty <div id="root">).

4.1 TEST WITH URL INSPECTION — VIEW TESTED PAGE
  In GSC URL Inspection → "Test Live URL" → "View Tested Page"
  This shows you EXACTLY what Googlebot sees when it renders your page.
  
  Check:
    a) Click "HTML" tab — Does it show your full rendered HTML with content?
       If you see only <div id="root"></div>, the JS failed to execute.
    b) Click "Screenshot" tab — Does it show your page visually?
       If screenshot shows a blank/empty page, JavaScript rendering failed.

4.2 IF JAVASCRIPT RENDERING FAILS:
  This is the SPA indexing problem described in the SEO Audit Report.
  SOLUTION: Implement react-snap prerendering (see FINAL_SEO_IMPLEMENTATION_REPORT.txt).
  
  With prerendering:
    The HTML tab will show full content immediately
    The screenshot will show the complete rendered page

4.3 TEST STRUCTURED DATA
  Google Search Console → Enhancements → Rich Results
  Or use the standalone tool: https://search.google.com/test/rich-results
  
  Enter each URL and verify:
    - WebApplication schema detected
    - FAQPage schema detected (on LED and Resistor pages)
    - No errors or warnings
  
  Fix any schema errors before requesting indexing — errors reduce eligibility
  for rich results in SERPs.


================================================================================
 STEP 5 — MONITOR INDEXING PROGRESS
================================================================================

5.1 COVERAGE REPORT (check weekly)
  GSC → Indexing → Pages
  
  WHAT TO WATCH:
    "Indexed" count — should grow over 2-4 weeks as Google discovers pages
    "Crawled - currently not indexed" — pages Google saw but didn't index yet
      (This is common for new sites. May take multiple crawl cycles to index.)
    "Discovered - currently not indexed" — Google knows about the page but
      hasn't crawled it yet. Submit sitemap again if this persists > 2 weeks.
    "Excluded by noindex" — Should be ZERO (verify no noindex meta tags exist)
    "Alternate page with proper canonical tag" — Normal for query-param variants
      (e.g., /resistor-color-code?bands=4 pointing to /resistor-color-code)

5.2 SEARCH PERFORMANCE REPORT (check weekly after first indexing)
  GSC → Performance → Search results
  
  Metrics to track:
    Total clicks       — Direct traffic from Google search
    Total impressions  — How many times your pages appeared in search results
    Average CTR        — Click-through rate (target: >3% for calculator tools)
    Average position   — Where you rank (position 1-10 = page 1 of Google)
  
  Filter by Page → Click each calculator page → See which keywords trigger it.
  
  Filter by Query → See which keywords are generating impressions.
  Use this data to inform your content strategy:
    If you see impressions but no clicks for a keyword, improve your title/description.
    If you see zero impressions for a target keyword, add more content targeting it.

5.3 PAGE EXPERIENCE REPORT
  GSC → Experience → Page Experience
  Monitor Core Web Vitals (LCP, CLS, FID/INP).
  Pages with "Good" status rank better than "Poor" pages.

 STEP 6 — FIX COMMON INDEXING ERRORS
================================================================================

6.1 "SOFT 404" — PAGE NOT FOUND BUT RETURNS 200 STATUS
  Cause: React SPA returns 200 OK for all routes, even non-existent ones,
  because Nginx serves index.html for all 404s.
  
  IMPACT: If Googlebot crawls a URL that doesn't exist in React Router
  (like /some-old-url), it gets a 200 response with a "Page Not Found" message.
  Google may classify this as a "soft 404."
  
  FIX: Ensure the 404 component in App.js is clearly identified:
    Add <meta name="robots" content="noindex" /> to the NotFound component
    via react-helmet-async. This tells Google not to index true 404 pages.
    
    In App.js NotFound component:
      import { Helmet } from 'react-helmet-async';
      function NotFound() {
        return (
          <>
            <Helmet>
              <meta name="robots" content="noindex, nofollow" />
              <title>404 – Page Not Found | PoolCalc</title>
            </Helmet>
            ... rest of component
          </>
        );
      }

6.2 "DUPLICATE WITHOUT USER-SELECTED CANONICAL"
  Cause: Multiple URLs serve similar content without canonical tags.
  FIX: Already addressed — canonical tags are set correctly in SEOHead.jsx.
  Monitor GSC for this error and resolve any new cases.

6.3 "CRAWLED - CURRENTLY NOT INDEXED"
  Cause: Google crawled the page but decided not to index it.
  Possible reasons:
    - Page content is too thin (add more text content)
    - Page is too similar to another page (canonical issue)
    - JavaScript rendering issues (implement prerendering)
    - Low quality signals (new domain, no backlinks)
  
  FIX:
    1. Use URL Inspection to see Google's rendered version
    2. If rendered content is thin, add more text to the page
    3. Build 2-3 quality backlinks to the page
    4. Wait 2-4 weeks and request re-indexing

6.4 "REDIRECT ERROR"
  Cause: Page redirects to an unexpected location.
  FIX: Check Nginx config and Cloudflare redirect rules. Ensure no redirect
  loops or chains. All redirects should be direct (A → B, not A → B → C).

6.5 "SERVER ERROR (5XX)"
  Cause: Origin server returning 500 errors.
  FIX: Check Nginx logs on the VPS. Ensure the React build is deployed
  correctly and the server has sufficient memory and resources.

 STEP 7 — ACCELERATE INDEXING: OFF-PAGE ACTIONS
================================================================================

These actions help Google find and trust your site faster. New domains
especially benefit from early signals.

7.1 SUBMIT TO BING WEBMASTER TOOLS
  URL: https://www.bing.com/webmasters/
  Bing also powers Yahoo! search. Submit your sitemap here too.
  Also enables import of GSC data for easier setup.

7.2 CREATE AND VERIFY A GOOGLE ANALYTICS 4 PROPERTY
  URL: https://analytics.google.com/
  Connect GA4 to your site by adding the gtag snippet to index.html.
  GA4 + GSC integration gives richer data in Search Console.

7.3 POST ON SOCIAL MEDIA (SIGNALS GOOGLE TO CRAWL)
  Share each calculator page URL on:
    - Reddit: r/electronics, r/arduino, r/rfelectronics, r/ECE
    - Twitter/X: with hashtags #electronics #arduino #PCB #engineering
    - Discord: Post in electronics/maker Discord servers (mention arpankar077)
  
  These social shares create crawlable links that Googlebot follows,
  accelerating initial discovery.

7.4 CREATE ONE QUALITY BACKLINK IMMEDIATELY
  The fastest indexing trick: get ONE quality link from an indexed site.
  Options:
    a) Post on dev.to or Medium.com with a link to your site
    b) Create a GitHub README that links to your live site
    c) Add your site to a "free tools" page on a relevant wiki
    d) Post on Quora answering "What's a good electronics calculator?"
       and link to your site
  
  Even one followed link from an already-indexed page causes Googlebot to
  follow the link and index your site much faster.

7.5 SUBMIT TO WEB DIRECTORIES
  Small impact but helps with discovery:
  - https://curatedapps.com (submit your app)
  - https://www.producthunt.com (launch your product)
  - https://alternativeto.net (list as alternative to Digi-Key calculators)
  - https://www.saashub.com/submit-product
  - GitHub Topics: Create a public repo with the topic "electronics-calculator"

================================================================================

8.1 GOOGLE SEARCH CONSOLE — FREE RANKING TRACKER
  GSC → Performance → Search Results
  Click "Queries" tab → Sort by "Position" ascending to see top rankings.
  
  Set date range to "Last 3 months" to see trends.

    Month 1:   10+ pages indexed, first impressions appearing
    Month 2:   50+ total impressions, some keywords in positions 20-50
    Month 3:   500+ impressions, 5+ keywords in top 20
    Month 6:   5,000+ impressions, 10+ keywords on page 1 (positions 1-10)
    Month 12:  20,000+ impressions, ranking for primary calculator keywords

8.2 FREE TOOLS FOR RANK TRACKING
  - Google Search Console (official, free, most accurate)
  - Ubersuggest (Neil Patel, free tier available)
  - Ahrefs Webmaster Tools (free for your own domain)
    Register at: https://ahrefs.com/webmaster-tools
    Provides backlink data, crawl errors, and keyword rankings for free.

8.3 MONITOR CORE WEB VITALS IN GSC
  GSC → Experience → Core Web Vitals
  Target: All pages in "Good" category (green)
  Pages with LCP < 2.5s, CLS < 0.1, INP < 200ms rank better.

8.4 WEEKLY CHECK ROUTINE (15 minutes per week)
  Monday:
    - Check GSC Performance for new clicks/impressions
    - Note any new keywords appearing in the Queries tab
  Wednesday:
    - Check Coverage report for new indexing errors
    - Resolve any new "Not indexed" pages
  Friday:
    - Spot-check 2-3 pages with URL Inspection
    - Verify new blog posts were indexed after publishing

 STEP 9 — GOOGLE ADSENSE INTEGRATION WITH SEARCH CONSOLE
================================================================================

9.1 LINK ADSENSE TO SEARCH CONSOLE
  After AdSense approval:
  GSC → Settings → Associations → Google AdSense
  Linking reveals which search queries bring traffic that generates ad revenue.
  This helps you invest content effort in the highest-revenue keywords.

9.2 VERIFY ADSENSE ADS.TXT
  After getting AdSense approval, Google provides an ads.txt file content.
  Create: public/ads.txt with content:
    google.com, pub-YOURPUBLISHERID, DIRECT, f08c47fec0942fa0
  
  Replace YOURPUBLISHERID with your actual publisher ID.
  This file MUST be accessible at:
    https://poolcalculator.electropool.online/ads.txt
  Without ads.txt, many premium advertisers won't bid on your inventory,
  reducing your RPM (revenue per thousand impressions) significantly.

 STEP 10 — INDEXING TIMELINE EXPECTATIONS
================================================================================

WEEK 1:
  - Submit sitemap
  - Request manual indexing for all 10 pages
  - Homepage should be indexed within 3-7 days
  - First impressions may appear in GSC within 1-2 weeks

WEEK 2-4:
  - Calculator pages start getting indexed
  - First organic clicks may appear (usually from long-tail, low-competition queries)
  - GSC shows impressions for branded queries (poolcalculator, poolcalc)

MONTH 2:
  - All pages should be indexed
  - First rankings on page 2-5 for low-competition keywords
  - Blog articles (if published) start getting indexed

MONTH 3-6:
  - Rankings improve as Google assesses engagement signals
  - Long-tail keywords (KD < 25) should reach page 1
  - Primary keywords (KD 35-45) start moving to pages 2-3

MONTH 6-12:
  - Consistent organic traffic building
  - Target: 1,000-5,000 monthly visits from organic search
  - With blog content: target 5,000-20,000 monthly visits

IMPORTANT NOTE ON NEW DOMAINS:
  Google applies a "sandbox" effect to new domains. Even with perfect SEO,
  new sites typically see limited organic traffic for the first 3-6 months.
  This is normal. The sandbox period is shorter if:
  1. Your content is significantly better than existing results
  2. You build quality backlinks early
  3. Your CTR from impressions is high (compelling titles/descriptions)
  4. Users stay on your pages (low bounce rate, high engagement time)
